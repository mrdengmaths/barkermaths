How Graph Transformations Work

1 Translations (Dragging)

Dragging the shape moves it left/right or up/down. Notice how the equation changes:

  • Vertical Shift: Adding a number at the end moves it UP. y = x² + 3
  • Horizontal Shift: Subtracting a number inside the brackets moves it RIGHT. y = (x - 4)²

2 Reflecting over X-Axis

Clicking the red Reflect over X-Axis button grabs the horizontal grid line and flips the entire 3D plane over it.

Mathematically, this makes the entire equation negative: y = -( ... ).
If the shape was originally pointing up, it now points down!

3 Reflecting over Y-Axis

Clicking the green Reflect over Y-Axis button grabs the vertical grid line and flips the 3D plane like a book page.

Mathematically, this makes the inner x negative: y = (-x)².
Fun fact: Since negative numbers become positive when squared, a parabola centered at zero looks exactly the same after a Y-reflection!
